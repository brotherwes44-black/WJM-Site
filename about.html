Who I Am

A Scholar-Practitioner
Rooted in Community

Wesley J. Morris is a scholar, pastor, community organizer, and educator whose work spans more than two decades at the intersection of faith, liberation, and structural transformation. He brings a rare convergence of intellectual depth, spiritual grounding, and on-the-ground organizing experience to every engagement — whether in a university lecture hall, a denominational sanctuary, a grassroots coalition meeting, or an international solidarity tour.

Born and raised in the Black freedom struggle tradition, Wesley's formation was shaped from early adulthood by the mentorship of Rev. Nelson Johnson and the survivors of the 1979 Greensboro Massacre — one of the defining moments of 20th-century labor and civil rights history in the American South. That formation gave him both a framework and a fire: a commitment to truth-telling, structural analysis, and the patient, relational work of building Beloved Community.

Wesley holds a Master of Divinity from Union Theological Seminary in New York City, where he studied under Dr. James Cone and Dr. Cornel West — two of the most significant theologians of the 20th century. He is currently completing his doctoral studies in Education and Leadership at Fordham University's Graduate School of Education, where his dissertation explores Fugitive Christology and Liberatory Education. He is a graduate of NC A&T State University, where he studied History.

He serves as Senior Pastor of Faith Community Church in Greensboro, NC — a justice-centered congregation committed to prophetic witness, community care, and the Beloved Community tradition. He also serves as Vice President of Programs at Southern Vision Alliance, where he oversees grantmaking strategy and program development across three social justice funds supporting communities throughout the US South.

Wesley's work has taken him across four continents — Palestine, Ghana, Tanzania, Cuba, Brazil, India, Belize, Barbados, and Ethiopia among them. In 2017, he and his wife Kenya summited Uhuru Peak, Mt. Kilimanjaro (19,341 ft) — an ascent that became the origin of the UHURU Framework™. He is a recipient of the Aspen Institute Leadership Fellowship, the Lilly Foundation Compelling Preaching Grant, and the NC NAACP's Martin Luther King Jr. Youth Organizer Self-Determination Award, among many others.

Framework One

The UHURU
Framework™

A Mountain-Based Model for Change

Uhuru — the Swahili word for freedom — is the name of the summit of Mt. Kilimanjaro and the destination of Wesley's 2017 climb, inspired by his wife Kenya's vision to make the hike via the Machame Route. At 19,341 ft, reaching Uhuru required moving through five distinct climate zones, in the dark, toward a place they could not yet see.

That climb became the architecture of a change model Wesley has spent years developing and refining across pastoral, organizational, and educational contexts. The word pole pole (poh-lay poh-lay) — "slowly, slowly" — became a mantra on the mountain and a gift for every transformational journey since.

"Getting to the peak requires an appreciation of the dark as we move to a place we cannot yet see."
— Wesley J. Morris

The UHURU Framework™ is not a linear ladder. It is a terrain-responsive map for individuals, organizations, and communities navigating profound change.

The Five Zones

U Zone 01
Uncover
The Rainforest — Dense, humid, alive

The container-building phase. The work here is naming what is carried: the histories, the wounds, the assumptions, the grief. The facilitator's task is to help people recognize what they are actually working with before beginning to work with it.

Guiding question: What are we actually carrying into this?
H Zone 02
Hold
The Moorland — Open, exposed, wind-swept

The Moorland is where cover is gone and full exposure of the terrain becomes visible. The hidden becomes seen — and this is exactly where fear, deception, and unexpressed anger surface. The central task is to hold the space without rushing it.

Guiding question: Can we hold this without collapsing or escaping?
U Zone 03
Unbind
The Semi-Desert — The paradox zone

The most paradoxical terrain on the mountain. The Machame Route descends here before it ascends — a move that looks, from the outside, like retreat or failure. Pole pole. This is where acclimatization happens. What appears to be regression is often the most critical preparation for the climb ahead.

Guiding question: What must be released so the ascent can happen?
R Zone 04
Rise
The Alpine Desert — The Barranco Wall

The Alpine Desert culminates in the Barranco Wall — a sheer volcanic rock face that appears impassable from below. Once you are on it, it becomes navigable. One handhold at a time. The terror was real. The wall was climbable. Both things are true.

Guiding question: What does the path forward actually look like from here?
U Zone 05
Uhuru
The Summit — 19,341 ft

The final push begins at midnight. Six hours in darkness. The air is thin. The summit is invisible. But the climber has been in the dark before — and has learned to move toward a place not yet seen. Then: Stella Point. The crater rim. The first light of dawn. Freedom.

Guiding question: What do we carry back with us from the summit?

Standalone Module · Within the UHURU Framework™

The Descent
Module

What happens after the summit? Every climber knows it — the descent is its own journey, and no one tells you how hard it is. The Descent Module is a standalone, post-summit process for individuals and groups who have done the UHURU work and need dedicated support for what comes next: re-entry, integration, and the long walk back to the gate.

Drawing on Moses' descent from Sinai, the Transfiguration, and the raising of Lazarus, the module provides a theological framework for the post-summit void — addressing the three great temptations of return, a four-phase descent process, and a 30/60/90 day integration framework.

Phase I · Crater Rim
Phase II · Long Trail
Phase III · Forest Returns
Phase IV · The Gate

— Mark 9:9

The Descent Module is available as a standalone workshop following any UHURU engagement, or as an add-on session within the 2-day intensive format. It includes contextual applications for organizations, couples, and individuals.

Framework Two

The Luminous
Dark™

A Personal Development & Consciousness-Raising Journey

There is a place in western Belize — deep in the limestone hills of the Cayo District — where the dark is not simply the absence of light. It is a presence. Wesley Morris entered that cave as part of a journey through the ancient underground river system at Jaguar Paw. Approximately a quarter of a mile in, the guide gave the instruction: turn off your headlamp.

What followed was unlike any darkness he had experienced above ground. This darkness had texture. It pressed against the skin. It was palpable — a darkness that could be felt, not just seen. And in that darkness, Wesley Morris did not experience fear. He experienced comfort.

That moment became the origin of a conviction: the darkness is not the enemy of growth. It is one of its most honest and most generative conditions. The Luminous Dark is built from that moment.

"The luminous dark is not darkness escaped. It is darkness inhabited fully enough to reveal what was always shining inside it."
— Wesley J. Morris, The Luminous Dark™

For Leaders · Professionals · Couples

You have built things. You have achieved things. And you are aware — perhaps more acutely than the people around you — that your external accomplishments have outpaced your internal development. This journey is not away from the dark. It is through it.

The Six Stages

Each stage corresponds to a chamber in the Belize cave system — a specific quality of darkness, a specific gift, a specific practice.

1 Stage
The Descent
Entering the Dark

The moment the headlamp goes off. The willingness to stop performing, stop explaining, and stop managing the image of yourself long enough to encounter what is actually present. Stage 1 does not require resolution. It requires only presence.

Who are you when no one is watching?
2 Stage
The Cold House
The Limits of Certainty

The stage of isolation — the discovery that the certainties you brought into the dark do not function here. The theologies that worked in the light, the relational scripts that held in easier seasons — in the cold of Stage 2, they rattle. They do not warm you.

What did you believe before you entered the dark that you are no longer sure about?
3 Stage
The Jaguar House
What Can See You in the Dark

The Jaguar House is the stage of being seen — by your own patterns, your own wounds, your own history, and the systems that formed you. The jaguar sees in the dark with perfect clarity. It does not need your headlamp.

What has been shaping you in the dark that you are only now beginning to name?
4 Stage
The Bat House
Chaos and the Threshold Moment

Disorienting, loud, moving in every direction at once. The liminal stage: the old self has been sufficiently dismantled that its familiar certainties are gone, but the new self has not yet fully formed. This is the threshold — the most dangerous and most generative point in the journey.

What is being born in you that requires this chaos to exist?
5 Stage
The Razor House
The Cost of Transformation

The stage of discernment and release — the honest reckoning with what must be cut away in order for genuine transformation to occur. Not everything survives the journey through the dark. Not everything should. Stage 5 is where participants discover how much lighter they are capable of being.

What is the difference between what you are being asked to release and what you actually are?
6 Stage
The Luminous Dark
Emergence and Integration

Stage 6 is not the end of darkness. It is the stage in which the participant has learned to see from within it. The cave is not left behind — it is carried forward as a source of authority and wisdom. The quiet authority of someone who has been somewhere real and come back with something true.

Who is waiting for your testimony?

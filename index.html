Wesley Morris is a scholar-practitioner whose work spans more than two decades at the intersection of community transformation, leadership development, and liberatory education. He brings a rare convergence of intellectual depth, spiritual grounding, and on-the-ground organizing to every engagement.

Ordained as Senior Pastor of Faith Community Church in Greensboro, NC, Wesley holds a Master of Divinity from Union Theological Seminary — where he studied under Dr. Cornel West and Dr. James Cone — and is completing doctoral studies in Education and Leadership at Fordham University.

Shaped by the mentorship of Rev. Nelson Johnson and the survivors of the 1979 Greensboro Massacre, his formation in the Beloved Community tradition gives him both a framework and a fire: a commitment to truth-telling, structural analysis, and the long, patient work of building just communities.

A Proprietary Model

The UHURU Framework™

A mountain-based model for change, challenge, and transition — drawn from the five climate zones of Mt. Kilimanjaro.

Uhuru — the Swahili word for freedom — names both the summit Wesley reached in 2017 and the destination of every transformational journey he guides. At 5,895 meters, the climb required moving through five distinct zones, in the dark, toward a place not yet visible.

That ascent became the architecture of a change model refined across pastoral, organizational, and educational contexts for nearly a decade.
